TI The Analysis of Sub-Pixel/Sub-Pixel Spatial Attraction-Based Sub-Pixel Mapping Model
SO 2014 INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE ON GIS AND RESOURCE MANAGEMENT (ICGRM)
LA English
DT Proceedings Paper
DE Remote sensing image; Super-pixel mapping; Sub-pixel/sub-pixel spatial attraction model (SSSAM); Weighted distance function
ID bp neural-network; images; scales
AB Sub-pixel mapping is an effective method to obtain the distribution information in mixed pixels in remote sensing image. Sub-pixel/sub-pixel spatial attraction model, a new developed simple and effective one, realizes sub-pixel mapping through computing spatial attraction between centre sub-pixel and neighboring sub-pixels. The model measures the strength of the spatial dependence with the weighted distance function, but the selection of this distance function needs further analysis. In this paper, three weighted distance functions including Inverse distance model (IDM), Exponential model (EM) and Gaussian Model (GM) are discussed. Some useful conclusions are drawn from the experiments that Inverse distance model has the worst accuracy, Exponential model and Gaussian model have the similar accuracy, and Gaussian model is more sensitive to changed model parameter.

TI Large Scale Systems and Fuzzy Cognitive Maps: A critical overview of challenges and research opportunities
SO ANNUAL REVIEWS IN CONTROL
LA English
DT Review
AB Large scale systems (LSS) have been traditionally characterized by a large number of variables, nonlinearities and uncertainties. Their decomposition into smaller, more manageable subsystems, possibly organized in a hierarchical structure, has been associated with intense and time - critical information exchange and with the need for efficient and coordination mechanisms. A critical overview of the different theories and algorithms for LSS is provided. The issue of system complexity has become transparent. As the complexity of such systems increase and the presence of uncertainties play a role on the performance of LSS and HMS, new system theoretic methods become more crucial and are urgently needed. Intelligent Systems (IS) and Fuzzy Cognitive Maps (FCM) theories are such new theoretic approaches in modeling Large Scale Dynamic Complex Systems (LSDCS). An FCM is based on fuzzy logic and Neural Networks. FCM integrates the accumulated experience and knowledge on the operation of the system, as a result of the methods by which it is constructed. The new theories of FCMs are reviewed and used to model LSS and Dynamical Hierarchical Control Systems. A number of applications in using FCM to model complex systems from industrial processes economics, energy, environment, health international relations and political developments are mentioned. New challenges and research opportunities are presented and discussed. (C) 2014 Elsevier Ltd. All rights reserved.

TI Land usage classification: a hierarchical neural network approach
SO JOURNAL OF AGRICULTURAL SCIENCE
LA English
DT Article
ID adaptation; management; systems
AB The classification of land usage in mountain grassland bovine areas is important for the management of forage production and grazing in grass-based livestock systems. The present paper proposes a novel, hierarchical neural network-based approach towards the classification of land usage in these areas. A survey of 72 farms was conducted in the Massif Central (France). Information was gathered on geographical characteristics and cutting and/or grazing practices on three general groups of fields: cut only, cut and grazed and grazed only fields. To classify land usage, the data were clustered and visualized in a hierarchical fashion. This was done by using a novel method for the analysis and classification of data based on growing hierarchical self-organizing maps (GHSOM). Self-organizing maps (SOM) have been shown to be successful for the analysis of highly dimensional input data in data mining applications as well as for data visualization. Moreover, the hierarchical architecture of the GHSOM is more flexible than a single SOM in the adaptation process to input data, capturing inherent hierarchical relationships among them. Experimental results show the utility of this approach.

TI Modeling Engineering-Geological Layers by k-nn and Neural Networks
SO NEURAL NETWORKS AND ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, ICNNAI 2014
LA English
DT Proceedings Paper
DE k-nn classifier; neural network approximation; engineering-geological layer; geological mapping; geological cartography
AB In this paper a novel approach for solving task of engineering geological layers approximation is described. The task is confined in performing smooth geological surface from the heterogenic array of points in 3-dimensional space. This approach is based on statistical learning systems, as: k-nn (k-nearest neighbors) algorithm and neural networks (multilayer perceptron) which allows to separate points belonging to different geological layers. Our method enables also modeling convex 3-dimensional intrusions. The main advantage of our approach is the possibility of the surface model scaling without increasing the calculation complexity.

TI A lossless DEM compression for fast retrieval method using fuzzy clustering and MANFIS neural network
SO ENGINEERING APPLICATIONS OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
LA English
DT Article
DE Data compression; DCR problem; Dem; Fuzzy clustering; Manfis
ID digital elevation maps; image compression; efficient search; model
AB In this paper, we propose an integrated approach between fuzzy C-means (FCM) and multi-active neuro fuzzy inference system (MANFIS) for the lossless DEM compression for fast retrieval (DCR) problem, aiming to compress digital elevation model (DEM) data with the priority of fast retrieval from the client machine over the Internet environment. Previous researches of this problem either used the float wavelet transforms integrated with the SPIHT coding or constructed a predictor model using statistical correlation of DEM data in local neighborhoods; thus giving large-sized compressed data and slow transferring time of data between the server and the client. Based on the observation that different non-linear transforms for predictive values in the sliding windows may increase the compression ratio, we herein present a novel approach for OCR problem and validated it experimentally on the benchmark DEM datasets. The comparative results show that our method produces better compression ratio than the relevant ones. (C) 2013 Elsevier Ltd. All rights reserved.
